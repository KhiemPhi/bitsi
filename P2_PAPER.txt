ABSTRACT
When has a robot seen enough OF THE OBSERVABLE SCENE? We accumulate views into a
MONOTONE FREE-SPACE filtration whose persistence diagram converges: if successive
surface samples are h_k-dense (Hausdorff), then bottleneck(D_k, D_inf) <= C*h_k
(Sec. 4), so the diagram converges to that of the observable reconstruction. The
retrospective increment bottleneck(D_k, D_{k-1}) is a measurable quantity; we use
it as a convergence-MOTIVATED stopping CRITERION (it upper-bounds the residual only
under a stated monotone-increment-decay condition, which we check empirically).
This is a DETERMINISTIC GEOMETRIC criterion -- about geometric stabilization, not
probabilistic risk control. The same persistence machinery, tracked across motion
as a vineyard, discovers articulated parts and joint types. Against next-best-view
baselines EQUIPPED WITH THEIR OWN STOPPING RULES, our criterion stops earlier at
equal task success [MOCK: 6.4 vs 9.8 views at 0.94 success]; task-success loss vs
exhaustive capture is [MOCK] median 2.3% (90th-pct 4.1%); joint-type accuracy is
[MOCK] 88% (training-free). "Certify" denotes geometric stabilization of the
observable reconstruction, not correctness of any downstream estimate.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. INTRODUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Active perception must decide when more views stop paying off. Next-best-view (NBV)
methods optimize an information-gain surrogate but typically stop on a heuristic
budget or gain threshold with no convergence statement. We give a stopping
criterion motivated by a convergence theorem with an explicit rate.

As views accumulate into a fused free-space representation, the set of voxels known
to be free GROWS MONOTONICALLY; the corresponding sublevel-set filtration is
genuinely nested, so its persistence diagram converges -- and, via the stability
theorem, at a rate set by surface-sample density. The increment between successive
reconstructions, bottleneck(D_k, D_{k-1}), is measurable and RETROSPECTIVE, so
thresholding it gives a terminating criterion free of optional-stopping pathology.
We are explicit that this increment upper-bounds the residual to D_inf only under a
stated decay condition; absent that, it is a convergence-motivated heuristic, and
we report how often the condition holds.

The same machinery generalizes across MOTION: tracking the diagram as a function of
an articulation parameter (a vineyard) identifies articulated parts and joint
types, training-free.

3. METHOD
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 Monotone free-space filtration. Each view carves known-free space into a fused
volume. We filter on the KNOWN-FREE-SPACE confidence (which only grows with views),
NOT the surface estimate (whose zero-crossing moves). Sublevel sets are therefore
genuinely nested across view index k.
3.2 Convergence and stopping. D_k = persistence diagram after k views. Nestedness +
boundedness give convergence; the stability theorem gives the rate (Sec. 4). We STOP
when bottleneck(D_k, D_{k-1}) < tau; this increment is computed from observed
reconstructions. Registration robustness: loop-closure/pose-graph jumps cause
non-local field changes; we use a median-of-windows increment to discount them.
3.3 Articulation via vineyards. Parameterize the filtration by articulation state
(joint angle / interaction time); track the diagram as a vineyard. A feature whose
birth/death moves coherently with the state is an articulated part; the geometry of
its vine (linear vs constant displacement) distinguishes prismatic vs revolute.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. THEORY (deterministic geometric)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Assumption A1 (dense capture). The view schedule drives the surface-sample Hausdorff
density h_k -> 0 on the OBSERVABLE region as views accumulate. This is a property of
the capture process, not delivered by the theorem; adversarial/degenerate schedules
can violate it.

Proposition 1 (convergence with rate). Under A1, with the accumulated free-space
filtration nested, for the distance-function filtration,
   bottleneck(D_k, D_inf^obs) <= C * h_k,
where D_inf^obs is the diagram of the observable reconstruction and C depends only
on ambient dimension. Hence D_k -> D_inf^obs as h_k -> 0. (Proof: stability of
persistence diagrams [Cohen-Steiner et al.'07] applied to the nested distance
function; the bound is the Hausdorff-to-bottleneck inequality.)

Definition (geometric-increment-decay). The increment sequence
e_k = bottleneck(D_k, D_{k-1}) satisfies GEOMETRIC decay if e_k <= rho^k * e_0 for
some rho < 1 (a checkable single-parameter form, fit per scene from observed
increments).

Proposition 2 (residual bound, conditional -- concrete g). Under geometric decay,
by the triangle inequality and the geometric series,
   bottleneck(D_k, D_inf^obs) <= sum_{j>k} e_j <= e_k * rho/(1-rho) =: g(e_k);
thus STOPPING at e_k < tau bounds the residual by g(tau) = tau * rho/(1-rho) -- a
NUMERIC bound. WITHOUT decay, e_k bounds nothing about D_inf^obs in general (a late
view may reveal a large feature); we then present the rule as a convergence-MOTIVATED
criterion and report (Sec. 5.3) the empirical fraction of trajectories on which
decay holds.

Scope. A geometric stabilization statement about the OBSERVABLE reconstruction; NOT
distribution-free risk control and not a claim of task sufficiency. "Certify" =
the observable reconstruction has geometrically stabilized.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Convergence is to the OBSERVABLE reconstruction (undersides never observable);
"seen enough" is relative to the observable set. The residual bound (Prop 2) holds
only under the decay condition, which fails on a minority of trajectories (5.3).
Monotonicity holds on free-space, not the surface estimate. TDA exposition needs
care for a general audience.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. CONCLUSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A monotone free-space filtration turns "have I seen enough of the observable scene?"
into a convergence-motivated, terminating, deterministic-geometric stopping
criterion with an explicit rate; the same persistence machinery, tracked across
motion, discovers articulation without training.
