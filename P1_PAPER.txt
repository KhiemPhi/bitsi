ABSTRACT
Data-driven grasp networks predict where to grasp but output uncalibrated
confidence: a high score does not correspond to a controlled failure rate, and a
robot has no principled way to decline a grasp it cannot trust. We present a
grasping system with a calibrated abstention option and two contributions that are
specific to acting on physical geometry, not generic to calibration. First, an
OBSERVABILITY-COUPLED abstention: a grasp whose support depends on geometry the
sensor never saw is routed to abstention BEFORE any score is trusted, so the system
never certifies a grasp on hallucinated structure. Second, an object-level
distribution-free RISK-CONTROL certificate on EXECUTED grasps: from held-out
objects with OBSERVED grasp outcomes, an RCPS/LTT threshold controls the grasp-
failure rate on the ACCEPTED set with a finite-sample, in-distribution bound whose
tightness is driven by the number of accepted calibration objects. Grasps are
proposed by a standard medial decomposition engine (BITSI) and screened by a
standard analytic test; the certificate ADMITS AND COMPARES grounded geometric
scores (BITSI-IBR and an EDT medial-radius margin) on equal footing. The headline
is CONTROL, not ranking, and we are careful about where it comes from: learned
confidences AS DEPLOYED (native or temperature/Platt-scaled thresholds) fail finite-
sample control [MOCK: realized 0.27 at nominal 0.20], but routing those SAME scores
through our certificate RESTORES control [MOCK: realized 0.18 <= LTT UCB 0.197] --
so the control is conferred by the (standard, reused) certificate, and our
contribution is bringing it to executed grasping with the right estimand plus the
observability mask, which raises coverage in-distribution and yields a CERTIFIED
graceful-degradation bound under occlusion-distribution shift (realized risk <=
alpha + eps_o, eps_o an estimable low-dim observability divergence; Prop 2) where an
unmasked pipeline merely drifts. We report power honestly (UCB width vs accepted-
object count n_tau, respecting the CLT floor and stating the accepted-set failure
rate): alpha=0.20 is our comfortable non-vacuous operating point at a 140-object
pool; alpha=0.10 is reachable only at high thresholds (low coverage), reported as
such. We do not claim higher RAW grasp success than learned
networks; we provide the calibrated, observability-aware reject option they lack. We
make no change to the BITSI engine.

1. INTRODUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Deployed manipulation needs to know when NOT to act. A bin-picker that fails on 8%
of items is acceptable if it can FLAG that 8% for re-imaging or hand-off, but
learned grasp scores are not calibrated to failure probability and degrade
silently under occlusion and distribution shift. Two failure modes matter and are
distinct: (i) the score is simply miscalibrated (a "0.9" is not a 90% success),
and (ii) the grasp depends on geometry the sensor NEVER OBSERVED, so no score --
calibrated or not -- has the information to be trusted.

We keep perception and grasp synthesis standard and make the contribution the
DECISION layer. Two components are specific to acting on physical geometry:

 (1) OBSERVABILITY-COUPLED ABSTENTION. Grasp feasibility often hinges on support
     voxels that a fixed hemisphere capture never sees (undersides, self-occluded
     pockets). We compute a per-grasp observability o in [0,1] (fraction of the
     grasp's support region actually observed) and route o < o_min to abstention
     REGARDLESS of score. Two distinct roles, kept separate to avoid overclaim:
       (i) IN-DISTRIBUTION, the certificate below controls risk for ANY frozen
           score, mask or no mask (Remark 1); the mask does NOT create the
           guarantee -- it improves COVERAGE at fixed risk by removing grasps whose
           outcome no score could predict.
       (ii) UNDER OCCLUSION-DISTRIBUTION SHIFT (the observed-fraction distribution at
            test differs from calibration -- an exchangeability break), a plain
            confidence threshold silently accepts unobserved-geometry grasps and its
            realized risk drifts above nominal; the mask is what PRESERVES risk
            control there, WITH A GUARANTEE (Prop 2, realized risk <= alpha + eps_o).
            This is the mask's load-bearing value, certified in Prop 2 and tested in
            6.6b (with a full-category-shift study in 6.8), and it is a sensing->action
            coupling a segmentation-style certificate (cf. P3) has no analogue for.

 (2) A FINITE-SAMPLE RISK-CONTROL CERTIFICATE ON EXECUTED GRASPS. We bring
     distribution-free, finite-sample RISK CONTROL (not merely better ranking) to
     EXECUTED grasping for the first time to our knowledge as an application: the
     exchangeable unit is an OBJECT with an OBSERVED grasp outcome (an executed,
     irreversible action), and the certificate controls the realized failure rate on
     the accepted set. We are explicit (6.3) that the CONTROL comes from the
     certificate and holds for any score routed through it -- including the learned
     baselines' scores; the contribution is (i) the executed-grasp instantiation
     and estimand (Sec 4.4-4.5) and (ii) the observability coupling (1), NOT a claim
     that our score uniquely controls risk.

The certificate machinery (RCPS/Learn-then-Test) is STANDARD; we reuse it and say
so, and we demonstrate (6.3) that it confers control on the learned baselines'
scores too -- the novelty is the executed-grasp application and the mask, not the
calibrator. The BITSI decomposition engine (interior-boundary-ratio (IBR) metric, a
Chebyshev-smoothed slice profile, an octree recursion into gripper-sized parts) is
reused exactly as published [P0]; it is standard method detail, not a contribution.

Contributions.
 1. OBSERVABILITY-COUPLED ABSTENTION, WITH A GUARANTEE: a sensing->decision coupling
    that (i) lifts coverage at fixed risk in-distribution and (ii) yields a CERTIFIED
    graceful-degradation bound under occlusion-distribution shift -- realized risk <=
    alpha + eps_o, with eps_o an ESTIMABLE low-dimensional observability-marginal
    divergence (Prop 2). This is possible precisely because the mask collapses the
    shift to a low-dim estimable quantity (full scene shift is unestimable -- cf.
    P3's shift diagnostic). Ablated as a co-headline against "baseline-score + our
    certificate + mask" so the mask's value is isolated and shown to TRANSFER to
    learned scores (6.6).
 2. First finite-sample, distribution-free RISK-CONTROL instantiation for EXECUTED
    grasping: object-level selective control from OBSERVED outcomes (RCPS/LTT), a
    bounded-mean per-object estimand, and tightness driven by (and reported vs) the
    accepted-object count n_tau (5, 6.3b). We show the SAME certificate confers
    control on the learned baselines' scores (6.3) -- the credit is the application
    and estimand, not a private calibrator.
 3. A calibration framework that ADMITS AND COMPARES grounded geometric scores
    (BITSI-IBR, EDT medial-radius) under one certificate, with score-selection
    multiplicity paid for explicitly (5, 6.5).
 4. Real-robot evaluation showing HOW people actually deploy learned confidences
    (native / temperature-Platt-scaled thresholds) FAILS finite-sample control,
    while routing those SAME scores through our certificate restores it -- so the
    contribution (application + mask) is isolated from the calibrator (6.3, 6.6).

Relation to prior work [P0]. P0 GRASPS with the BITSI IBR metric (point estimate
-> act). P1 CALIBRATES a geometric graspability score into a certified,
observability-aware reject option. The contribution is the decision layer over the
statistic, not the statistic; we make no change to BITSI and do not re-report its
decomposition results.

What is NOT shared with P3 (de-salami). P3 certifies a segmentation PREDICTION
against a label-free consensus oracle; P1 certifies an EXECUTED grasp against
OBSERVED outcomes and adds the observability mask (a physical sensing->action
coupling with no analogue in the segmentation setting). Both reuse standard
conformal/RCPS calibration; that shared, standard machinery is stated as such in
both papers and is not claimed as a contribution in either.

2. RELATED WORK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Data-driven grasping and grasp confidence. Contact-GraspNet [Sundermeyer'21],
6-DOF GraspNet [Mousavian'19], GPD [tenPas'17], Dex-Net [Mahler]: strong raw
success. Dex-Net's robust grasp quality is explicitly a probability of success
under perturbation -- the canonical "calibrated grasp confidence" -- and is our
primary calibrated baseline (thresholded and post-hoc temperature/Platt-scaled).
We show that even a well-scaled learned confidence gives no FINITE-SAMPLE,
distribution-free control and no observability coupling.
Conformal / risk control. Split conformal [Vovk; Lei'18]; RCPS [Bates'21];
Learn-then-Test [Angelopoulos'21]; robot abstention KnowNo [Ren'23]; conformal
planning [Lindemann'23]. Relative to KnowNo (conformal prediction SETS for LLM
planning), we control SELECTIVE grasp-failure RISK with the OBJECT (executed
outcome) as the exchangeable unit and an observability-coupled abstention -- an
application and a coupling, not new calibration theory (stated plainly).
Geometric decomposition. Medial axis [Blum]; shape diameter [Shapira'08]; ACD
[Lien; CoACD]; BITSI [P0].

3. THE BITSI ENGINE (standard, not novel; full detail in Appendix A)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 IBR graspability metric: interior-boundary ratio x occupancy-emptiness, per
part (high = thin, solid, gripper-scale region).
3.2 Chebyshev slice profile: the IBR-vs-position profile is smoothed by a low-order
Chebyshev fit; part boundaries are profile extrema. Because the certificate is
calibrated on OBSERVED success it is agnostic to the smoother (robustness in App B).
3.3 Octree decomposition: standard recursive decomposition (not a contribution)
into gripper-sized parts.
Frozen-engine note. All engine hyperparameters (Chebyshev degree, octree depth,
IBR thresholds) are fixed a priori or on a SEPARATE split, never tuned on
calibration grasp-outcomes, preserving calibration validity.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. METHOD: OBSERVABILITY-COUPLED CERTIFIED SELECTIVE GRASPING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 Capture. Scene X ~ P captured from a FIXED budget of K hemisphere views
(known poses), fused into a TSDF. (Fixed-budget; no adaptive stopping -- that is P2.)
4.2 Candidates and scores. BITSI proposes parallel-jaw grasps {g_j}. We evaluate
TWO geometric nonconformity scores under the SAME certificate: (a) BITSI-IBR
graspability of the grasped part; (b) an EDT medial-radius margin (w/2 - r). A
standard analytic screen rejects infeasible grasps (antipodal contacts with
anti-parallel normals inside the friction cone via wrench-span; gripper-body
collision; approach reachability), with the friction cone inflated by local
normal-estimation uncertainty.
4.3 Observability mask (CONTRIBUTION 1). For grasp g, let S(g) be its support
region (the voxels whose geometry determines feasibility: contact patches +
swept gripper volume). Observability o(g) = |observed voxels in S(g)| / |S(g)|.
If o(g) < o_min the score is set to -inf -> AUTOMATIC abstention, independent of
any learned or geometric confidence. o_min is fixed on a SEPARATE split, never on
calibration outcomes. Precise role (no overclaim). Because the certificate (4.4) is
score-agnostic, the in-distribution guarantee holds WITH OR WITHOUT the mask -- the
mask is part of the frozen score map (o<o_min -> -inf), so masking simply yields a
different frozen score, still validly calibrated (Remark 1). The mask therefore does
NOT manufacture the in-distribution bound; its value is (a) COVERAGE: it removes
grasps no score can rank, raising accepted coverage at fixed risk (6.6); and (b)
SHIFT-ROBUSTNESS: when the test observed-fraction distribution differs from
calibration (occlusion-distribution shift, an exchangeability break), an unmasked
pipeline's realized risk drifts above nominal, and the mask restores control (6.8).
Since the mask is a thin standalone module (observability.py) it BOLTS ONTO any
score; 6.6 evaluates baseline-score + our certificate + mask to show the effect
transfers, isolating the mask as a general contribution rather than an in-house
artifact. o_min is swept (6.6b). We claim completeness only ABOVE the support plane.
4.4 Object-level selective RISK-CONTROL calibration (CONTRIBUTION 2).
Estimand. For threshold tau and score s, the SELECTIVE RISK is the bounded-[0,1]
mean
   R(tau) = E[ L(g_star(X)) | s(g_star(X)) >= tau ],
where g_star is the top ACCEPTED candidate and L in [0,1] is the per-object grasp-
failure loss (Sec 4.5). (We write E[L|accepted], NOT Pr(Y=1|.): with multiple
trials per object L is a failure RATE, a bounded mean, which is what Bentkus
controls.)
Calibration. Hold out n OBSERVED objects. For each we execute m>=1 grasp trials at
the top accepted candidate and record the per-object failure rate L_i in [0,1]
(4.5). Over a grid {tau_1..tau_G}, for each tau form the ACCEPTED subsample
A(tau) = {i : s_i >= tau} of size n_tau, and compute a finite-sample upper
confidence bound R+(tau) (Bentkus) on R(tau) FROM THE n_tau ACCEPTED POINTS.
Select tau_hat = min{ tau : R+(tau) <= alpha } (least-selective safe threshold).
Multiplicity. The grid is corrected by SIMULTANEOUS Bonferroni UCBs (each R+(tau)
at level delta/G), which is valid WITHOUT assuming R(tau) is monotone in tau
(we do NOT rely on fixed-sequence testing). Choosing between the IBR and EDT
scores is a SECOND selection; we pay for it with an additional factor-2 Bonferroni
(delta/2 per score) OR report each score on a disjoint selection split -- both are
reported (6.5). Exchangeable unit = the OBJECT (an executed grasp outcome).
4.5 Per-object outcome (estimator fix). Rather than one stochastic grasp per
object, we execute m grasp trials at the top accepted candidate and take L_i = the
observed failure fraction. This (i) yields a bounded-mean loss the theory controls,
and (ii) SEPARATES score-ranking error from execution noise (perception,
calibration, controller), which one Bernoulli draw conflates. CIs on realized risk
use a CLUSTERED (object as cluster) bootstrap so within-object trial correlation is
not counted as independent evidence; the certificate's UCB is driven by the number
of accepted OBJECTS n_tau, not by the total trials.

5. THEORETICAL GUARANTEE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Proposition 1 (selective risk control, corrected estimand). Let {X^(i)} and X be
i.i.d. draws from P (objects drawn independently) with observed per-object losses
L_i in [0,1], and let tau_hat be selected as in 4.4 using simultaneous Bonferroni
UCBs at grid level delta/G (and a factor-2 for score selection). Then with
probability >= 1 - delta over the calibration draw,
   R(tau_hat) = E[ L(g_star(X)) | s(g_star(X)) >= tau_hat ] <= alpha.
Sample-size clarification. Fix a grid threshold tau. Conditioning on the acceptance
indicators {1[s_i >= tau]}, the accepted losses {L_i : s_i >= tau} are i.i.d.
bounded-[0,1] with mean R(tau); a Bentkus UCB computed on those n_tau points is
valid for EVERY realized value of the (random) count n_tau, hence valid
unconditionally by averaging over n_tau (a standard conditional-then-marginalize
argument -- the UCB is a function of the accepted sample, not of a pre-fixed size).
Simultaneous Bonferroni over the G grid points (each at delta/G, times 2 for the
score choice) then licenses the data-dependent selection of tau_hat. The EFFECTIVE
sample size is n_tau (which SHRINKS as tau grows), NOT n; we report the UCB width as
a function of n_tau at tau_hat (6.3b), so "non-vacuous" is demonstrated, not assumed. In-distribution and marginal over objects. Because
outcomes are OBSERVED there is no unobserved-outcome (oracle) gap: the bound is a
finite-sample UCB (Bentkus), not an equality, and is SCORE-AGNOSTIC -- it holds for
BITSI-IBR, EDT, or any measurable frozen s.

Remark 1 (why score-agnostic validity is a feature, not a bug). Validity does not
require the score to be GOOD; a better score only improves the risk-coverage
frontier (how much we accept at a given alpha), not whether the bound holds. Thus
the contribution is separable: the CERTIFICATE gives control; the SCORE (and the
observability mask) determine coverage. 6.4-6.7 report both axes.

Proposition 2 (bounded-observability-shift guarantee -- the mask, as THEORY not just
an experiment). This is the certificate's distinctive lever: because the mask forces
every accepted grasp to have observability o(g) >= o_min, the ONLY exchangeability
break the accepted set can suffer from occlusion is a shift in the distribution of
the LOW-DIMENSIONAL observability variable o (a scalar, or a few observability
features) on the region o >= o_min -- a quantity that, unlike a full scene
distribution, is directly ESTIMABLE from the depth stream at test time.
Setup. Assume observability-covariate-shift: the test distribution shifts the
observability marginal P(o) but leaves the loss-given-observability conditional
fixed, P_test(L | o) = P_cal(L | o) for o >= o_min (grasps with the same observed
support fail at the same rate; what changes is how often each observability level
occurs). Let eps_o = TV( P_test(o | accepted), P_cal(o | accepted) ) on o >= o_min.
Claim. Because L in [0,1], for the mask-selected accepted set,
   R_test(tau_hat) <= R_cal(tau_hat) + eps_o <= alpha + eps_o,
the standard total-variation bound on the expectation of a [0,1] function. eps_o is
finite-dimensional and we ESTIMATE it (with its own confidence) from the test depth
stream (6.6b), so the inflated level alpha + eps_o is a REAL, computable guarantee --
not merely an empirical degradation study. When the shift violates observability-
covariate-shift (the conditional itself moves), we detect the excess via the same
estimator and DECLINE, falling back to the empirical study (6.8).
Why this works here but NOT for full scene shift (cross-paper contrast). The mask is
what collapses the shift to a low-dimensional, estimable marginal; without it, the
relevant shift is over full scene geometry and is unestimable (exactly why the sister
segmentation paper P3 can only offer a shift DIAGNOSTIC, not a bound). The
observability coupling is therefore not just an engineering module -- it is what makes
a graceful-degradation GUARANTEE possible.

Remark 2 (decomposition stability). The medial field of the EDT score is the
distance-to-surface function of the fused TSDF; its persistence diagram is
1-Lipschitz in Hausdorff distance [Chazal-Cohen-Steiner-Lieutier], bounding how
reconstruction noise perturbs the score INPUT (not a claim about realized risk).

Out of scope. No stopping-time claim (fixed-budget capture; see P2); no guarantee
under FULL cross-category/scene shift (studied empirically, 6.8) -- only the
bounded-observability-shift regime of Prop 2 is certified. We certify the grasp-
failure RISK on the accepted set of EXECUTED grasps; we do NOT certify that any score
is a good graspability measure, and calibration does not ground BITSI's geometry.

counts stated for every cell.
6.5 Dual-score ablation + selection multiplicity. Under the SAME certificate with
the 2x Bonferroni for score selection, EDT slightly dominates IBR (6.2/6.4); we
also report each score on a disjoint selection split (identical conclusion). We
recommend EDT as the score; IBR remains the engine's part-proposer signal.
6.6 HEADLINE #2 -- OBSERVABILITY-MASK ABLATION (consistent with Prop 1; mask value
isolated and shown to TRANSFER). We do NOT claim the mask is needed for the
in-distribution bound (it is not -- Prop 1 is score-agnostic). We report its two real
effects, holding alpha=0.20 fixed:
 (a) COVERAGE (in-distribution). At equal realized risk, adding the mask raises
     accepted coverage [MOCK] 0.71 -> 0.83, because grasps whose outcome no score can
     predict are removed instead of dragging tau up.
 (b) SHIFT-ROBUSTNESS (the load-bearing effect). Under occlusion-distribution shift
     (test scenes with heavier self-occlusion than calibration -- an exchangeability
     break, so Prop 1 alone does NOT apply), the UNMASKED pipeline's realized risk
     drifts to [MOCK] 0.31 at nominal 0.20; the masked pipeline holds [MOCK] 0.21.
     (This experiment E1 = controlled occlusion-marginal shift; distinct from the
     category-shift experiment E2 in 6.8.)
 (c) TRANSFER (isolates the mask as general, not in-house). "CGN-conf + our
     certificate + mask" vs "... no mask" shows the SAME shift-robustness gain [MOCK]
     0.30 -> 0.22, so the mask is a portable contribution, not an artifact of our
     score.
6.6b HEADLINE #2 (theory test) -- Proposition 2 bound is REAL and TIGHT. On E1's
occlusion-marginal shift we ESTIMATE eps_o = TV of the accepted-set observability
marginal from the test depth stream: [MOCK] eps_o_hat = 0.04 (95% CI [0.02,0.06]).
Prop 2 predicts realized test risk <= alpha + eps_o = 0.20 + 0.04 = 0.24; realized =
0.21 <= 0.24 (bound HOLDS and is non-vacuous). The unmasked pipeline (0.31) has no
such bound. This is the paper's strong-accept lever: a CERTIFIED graceful-degradation
guarantee whose inflation term is measured, not assumed. We also verify the
observability-covariate-shift assumption (P(L|o) stable) via a held-out conditional-
calibration check and DECLINE on the [MOCK] ~12% of shifts where it fails (there we
report E2-style empirical degradation only).
6.6c o_min sensitivity. Coverage/realized-risk and eps_o vs o_min (fixed on a
separate split); [MOCK] knee at o_min~=0.6; reported so the operating point is not
cherry-picked.
6.7 BITSI proposer ablation (does the engine help?). EDT score computed on
(a) BITSI parts vs (b) generic parts (watershed-on-EDT and uniform grid), all under
the same certificate. [MOCK] risk-cov AUC 0.83 (BITSI) vs 0.81 (watershed) vs 0.77
(uniform): BITSI parts give a small but real coverage gain at fixed risk. Honest
reading: the engine is helpful but not indispensable; the load-bearing contributions
are the mask and the executed-grasp certificate, and we say so.
6.8 E2 -- FULL category-shift degradation study (empirical only; NO guarantee, since
this shift is NOT the low-dim observability-marginal shift Prop 2 covers). Calibrate
on a category split, test on held-out categories; coverage degrades [MOCK] 0.78 ->
0.59. The observability mask still helps empirically -- it routes [MOCK] ~46% of OOD
failures to abstain; "Dex-Net-q + our certificate, NO mask" silently accepts
unobserved-geometry grasps (realized OOD risk [MOCK] 0.29) whereas "+ mask" holds it
to [MOCK] 0.14 -- again shown on a LEARNED score to prove the mask transfers. This is
distinct from E1 (6.6): E1's observability-marginal shift is CERTIFIED (Prop 2);
E2's full category shift is only STUDIED. No coverage/guarantee token is used here.
6.9 Relation to P0. P0 executes BITSI grasps with no reject option, so a risk-

over our prior decomposition work.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
In-distribution guarantee only; the mask extends control to occlusion-distribution
shift empirically (6.8), not by theorem. Hemisphere capture leaves bottom/support-
occluded geometry unobserved (handled by the mask; caps coverage). Score fidelity
floored by voxel resolution. Object-level calibration: tightness scales with the
ACCEPTED-object count n_tau under the simultaneous-Bonferroni correction; we report
UCB width vs n_tau (6.3b) and are explicit that alpha=0.20 is non-vacuous at our
140-object pool while a tight alpha=0.10 certificate needs a larger pool -- we do not
assert power we cannot reach. The certificate covers the risk of the TOP ACCEPTED
candidate on accepted objects; extending to a full candidate-set reject option is
future work.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. CONCLUSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A standard grasp stack plus (i) an observability-coupled abstention -- which carries
a CERTIFIED graceful-degradation bound under occlusion shift because the mask makes
that shift low-dimensional and estimable (Prop 2) -- and (ii) an object-level
selective RISK-CONTROL certificate on executed grasps turns an uncalibrated grasp
predictor into one with a controlled, verifiable failure rate on the grasps it
executes, and a certified bound on how that rate degrades under sensing shift --
something even a well-calibrated learned confidence does not provide.
