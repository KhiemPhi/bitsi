ABSTRACT
Data-driven grasp networks predict where to grasp but output uncalibrated
confidence: a high score does not correspond to a controlled failure rate, and a
robot has no principled way to decline a grasp it cannot trust. We present a
grasping system with a calibrated abstention option. From a fixed multi-view
capture fused into a TSDF, we propose grasps with a standard medial decomposition
engine (BITSI) and screen them with a standard analytic test; our contribution is
a calibration FRAMEWORK that turns a per-part graspability score into an
object-level selective-risk certificate, and that ADMITS AND COMPARES grounded
geometric scores under one certificate. Using held-out objects with OBSERVED
grasp outcomes, an RCPS/LTT threshold controls the failure rate on the ACCEPTED
set with a finite-sample, in-distribution bound. An observability mask routes
grasps that depend on unobserved geometry to abstention. The headline result is
CALIBRATION: realized risk tracks the nominal level on held-out objects [MOCK:
0.11 realized within the LTT bound at alpha=0.10]; under the certificate, an EDT
medial-radius score slightly outperforms the BITSI-IBR score [MOCK: risk-cov AUC
0.83 vs 0.80]. We do not claim higher RAW grasp success than learned networks; we
provide the calibrated reject option they lack. We make no change to the BITSI
engine; we calibrate its (and an alternative geometric) graspability score into a
reject option with controlled grasp-failure risk.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. INTRODUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Deployed manipulation needs to know when NOT to act. A bin-picker that fails on 8%
of items is acceptable if it can FLAG that 8% for re-imaging or hand-off, but
learned grasp scores are not calibrated to failure probability and degrade
silently under occlusion and distribution shift.

We keep perception and grasp synthesis standard, and make the contribution the
DECISION layer: a distribution-free certificate on executed grasps, an
observability-aware abstention, and a framework that lets DIFFERENT geometric
graspability scores be compared on equal footing under one certificate. We reuse
the BITSI decomposition engine (interior-boundary-ratio (IBR) metric, a
Chebyshev-smoothed slice profile, and an octree recursion into gripper-sized
parts) exactly as published; it is standard method detail, not a contribution.

Contributions.
 1. Object-level selective-risk certificate for grasping: finite-sample,
    in-distribution control of grasp-failure risk on the accepted set (RCPS/LTT)
    from OBSERVED outcomes.
 2. A calibration framework that admits and compares grounded geometric scores,
    instantiated on the BITSI-IBR metric and an EDT medial-radius score; we report
    which calibrates to a better risk-coverage frontier rather than assuming one.
 3. Observability-mask abstention: never act on geometry the sensor never saw.
 4. Real-robot evaluation in clutter; the prior method [P0] appears as a measured
    baseline, framed as a risk-CONTROL comparison (not a success-lift claim).

Relation to prior work [P0]. P0 GRASPS with the BITSI IBR metric (point estimate
-> act). P1 CALIBRATES a geometric graspability score into a certified reject
option (uncalibrated statistic -> calibrated reject). The contribution is the
certificate over the statistic, not the statistic; we make no change to BITSI and
do not re-report its decomposition results.

2. RELATED WORK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Data-driven grasping. Contact-GraspNet [Sundermeyer'21], 6-DOF GraspNet
[Mousavian'19], GPD [tenPas'17], Dex-Net [Mahler]: strong raw success,

planning [Lindemann'23]. We control SELECTIVE grasp-failure risk with the OBJECT
as exchangeable unit and an observability-coupled abstention.
Geometric decomposition. Medial axis [Blum]; shape diameter [Shapira'08]; ACD
[Lien; CoACD]; BITSI [P0].

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. THE BITSI ENGINE (standard, not novel; full detail in Appendix A)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 IBR graspability metric: interior-boundary ratio x occupancy-emptiness, per
part (high = thin, solid, gripper-scale region).
3.2 Chebyshev slice profile: the IBR-vs-position profile is smoothed by a low-order
Chebyshev fit; part boundaries are profile extrema. Chebyshev gives bounded minimax
(equioscillation) approximation error; because the certificate is calibrated on
OBSERVED success it is agnostic to the smoother (robustness in App. B).
3.3 Octree decomposition: standard recursive decomposition (not a contribution)
into gripper-sized parts.
Frozen-engine note. All engine hyperparameters (Chebyshev degree, octree depth,
IBR thresholds) are fixed a priori or on a SEPARATE split, never tuned on
calibration grasp-outcomes, preserving calibration validity.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. METHOD: CERTIFIED SELECTIVE GRASPING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 Capture. Scene X ~ P captured from a FIXED budget of K hemisphere views
(known poses), fused into a TSDF. (Fixed-budget; no adaptive stopping -- that is
P2.)
4.2 Candidates and scores. BITSI proposes parallel-jaw grasps {g_j}. We evaluate
TWO geometric nonconformity scores under the SAME certificate: (a) BITSI-IBR
graspability of the grasped part; (b) an EDT medial-radius margin (w/2 - r). A
standard analytic screen rejects infeasible grasps (antipodal contacts with
anti-parallel normals inside the friction cone via wrench-span; gripper-body
collision; approach reachability), with the friction cone inflated by local
normal-estimation uncertainty.
4.3 Observability mask. Part observability o in [0,1] = fraction of support voxels
actually observed; if o < o_min the score is -inf -> automatic abstention. We
never certify grasps on hallucinated geometry; completeness claimed only ABOVE the
support plane.
4.4 Object-level selective-risk calibration. Hold out n OBJECTS, each with one
executed grasp at the top candidate and observed binary failure Y. Over a grid of
thresholds tau, form a finite-sample upper confidence bound R+(tau) (Bentkus) on
the selective risk R(tau)=Pr(Y=1 | s_star>=tau); select tau_hat=min{tau:R+(tau)<=
alpha}, grid multiplicity-corrected per LTT. Exchangeable unit = object.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. THEORETICAL GUARANTEE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Proposition 1 (selective risk control). Let {X^(i)} and X be exchangeable draws
from P with observed outcomes, tau_hat selected as in 4.4 at level (alpha,delta).
Then with probability >= 1-delta over the calibration draw,
   R(tau_hat) = Pr(Y_star=1 | s_star(X) >= tau_hat) <= alpha.
In-distribution and marginal over objects. Because outcomes are OBSERVED, there is
no unobserved-outcome (oracle) gap: the bound is a finite-sample UCB (Bentkus),
not an equality, and is SCORE-AGNOSTIC -- it holds for the BITSI-IBR score, the
EDT score, or any measurable s, provided the score is frozen across cal/test and
units are per-object i.i.d. We report the realized risk on the ACCEPTED objects
together with the LTT-corrected UCB value at tau_hat and its CI width vs n.

Remark 1 (decomposition stability). The medial field of the EDT score is the
distance-to-surface function of the fused TSDF; its persistence diagram is
1-Lipschitz in Hausdorff distance [Chazal-Cohen-Steiner-Lieutier], bounding how
reconstruction noise perturbs the score INPUT.

Out of scope. No stopping-time claim (fixed-budget capture; see P2); no
cross-category guarantee (studied empirically, 6.7). We certify the grasp-failure
RISK on the accepted set; we do NOT certify that any score is a good graspability
measure, and calibration does not ground BITSI's geometry.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. EXPERIMENTS  ([MOCK] numbers illustrative)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1 Setup. Franka + parallel-jaw + wrist RealSense; K=12-view hemisphere, Open3D
TSDF fusion. 40 objects across 8 categories (object-level cal/test split);
isolated + clutter; >=300 real grasp attempts; alpha in {0.10,0.20}, delta=0.10.
Calibration unit = the OBJECT: the finite-sample UCB is driven by the number of
held-out CALIBRATION OBJECTS (reported per split), NOT by the 300 execution
attempts, which are trials used to estimate per-object outcomes.
Baselines run in their NATIVE configuration on the same scenes: Contact-GraspNet
(full network ranking + its own confidence), GPD, plus a confidence-threshold
ablation of our score.
6.2 Make-or-break: score vs real success. Spearman rho between score and observed
success. [MOCK] IBR rho=0.53; EDT rho=0.62. Both monotone -> usable; EDT stronger.
6.3 HEADLINE -- calibration. Realized selective risk on HELD-OUT objects vs nominal
alpha, with the LTT UCB and binomial CIs (n_accepted reported). [MOCK] alpha=0.10:
LTT UCB=0.118, realized=0.11 over 27 accepted objects; alpha=0.20: UCB=0.214,
realized=0.19. Realized tracks within the UCB -> the certificate is valid and
non-vacuous at n=40.
6.4 Risk-coverage frontier (secondary). Selective success vs coverage; ours
(EDT/IBR + mask) vs CGN/GPD in native config.
  Method                          RiskCov-AUC up   SelSucc@80%cov up
  GPD (native)                    0.71 [MOCK]      0.83 [MOCK]
  Contact-GraspNet (native)       0.75 [MOCK]      0.87 [MOCK]
  Ours (IBR score + mask)         0.80 [MOCK]      0.91 [MOCK]
  Ours (EDT score + mask)         0.83 [MOCK]      0.93 [MOCK]
Improvements reported with CIs; we claim a better risk-coverage frontier, not
higher raw success.
6.5 Dual-score ablation. EDT slightly dominates IBR under the same certificate
(6.2/6.4). We therefore PRESENT BOTH and recommend EDT as the score; IBR remains
the engine's part-proposer signal. (This is the framework's point: scores are
interchangeable inputs the certificate compares.)
6.6 Certificate robustness to engine hyperparameters. Risk-cov AUC vs octree
depth, gripper-width threshold, Chebyshev degree, smoother choice
(Chebyshev/spline/Gaussian), under depth noise + partial views. [MOCK] AUC varies
< 0.02 -> certificate robust to the engine.
6.7 OOD degradation study (empirical only; no guarantee language). Calibrate on a
category split, test on held-out categories; coverage degrades [MOCK] 0.78 -> 0.59;
the observability mask accounts for [MOCK] ~46% of OOD failures it routes to abstain.
6.8 Relation to P0. P0 executes BITSI grasps with no reject option, so a
risk-coverage comparison is not applicable (N/A). The certificate is orthogonal to
P0's point-estimate policy: it wraps a calibrated abstain around the same geometry.
We deliberately report NO head-to-head success/risk lift over P0 -- the contribution
is the certificate, not a delta over our prior decomposition work.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
In-distribution guarantee only. Hemisphere capture leaves bottom/support-occluded
geometry unobserved (handled by abstention; caps coverage). Score fidelity floored
by voxel resolution. Object-level calibration: tightness scales with object count;
we report UCB width vs n and use n=40 to keep the bound non-vacuous.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. CONCLUSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A standard grasp stack plus an object-level selective-risk certificate, an
observability-aware abstention, and a score-comparison framework turns an
uncalibrated grasp predictor into one with a controlled, verifiable failure rate
on the grasps it executes.
