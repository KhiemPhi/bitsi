PAPER P3 — GEOMETRY-CALIBRATED SELECTIVE SEGMENTATION
Venue: RA-L (rolling). Submit first (~Aug 2026). Score 7.5 / safe accept.
Reuse: hemisphere rig, SAM 2, cross-view transfer, YOLO11-seg, Open3D, MAPIE/TorchCP.
Firewall: pure segmentation — NO general theorem, NO grasping.
================================================================================

ABSTRACT (claim-exact)
  A robot captures hemispherical RGB-D with known poses; SAM masks fuse across
  views into a consensus that denoises per-view errors; a fast YOLO-seg student is
  distilled with NO human masks and equipped with calibrated abstention. Calibration
  controls risk w.r.t. the multi-view consensus; a small held-out labeled set
  provides a finite-sample upper bound UCB(delta) on the consensus-to-truth gap,
  yielding R_true <= R_consensus + UCB(delta). delta shrinks with view diversity
  down to a floor set by view-correlated SAM error. Consensus-based calibration
  matches annotation-based calibration on ScanNet/Replica; the segmenter runs
  real-time onboard.

CONTRIBUTIONS
  1. Multi-view geometric consensus as a label-free calibration oracle.
  2. Consensus-to-truth risk transfer: R_true <= R_consensus + UCB(delta), finite-
     sample, with explicit correlated-error floor on delta.
  3. Leakage-free wedge nonconformity score (angularly-buffered, view-disjoint).
  4. A real-time onboard selective segmenter trained with zero human masks.

CLAIMS LEDGER (claim | evidence required | FORBIDDEN)
  - Risk control w.r.t. consensus, marginal over object units
      | CRC on object-level units, frozen model, symmetric-difference loss
      | FORBIDDEN: "per-instance", "distribution-free truth coverage"
  - R_true <= R_consensus + UCB(delta)
      | finite-sample delta UCB on human set; realized risk <= alpha+UCB(delta) vs GT
      | FORBIDDEN: stating delta as a point estimate
  - delta decreases with views to a FLOOR
      | UCB(delta) width vs n and vs view-correlation rho; floor reported
      | FORBIDDEN: "delta -> 0", "approaches truth with more views"
  - Label-free training + calibration
      | no human masks in train/calib; human set ONLY bounds delta (state size)
      | FORBIDDEN: "no labels at all"
  - Matches SAM quality, real-time
      | onboard Jetson runtime + mask parity "within X mIoU/sym-diff of SAM on
        ScanNet", stratified by scene difficulty (incl. clutter)
      | FORBIDDEN: "YOLO >= SAM", "outperforms SAM", bare "matches"

METHOD + CODE REUSE
  - Hemisphere capture (known poses)            [reuse rig + Open3D]
  - Multi-view consensus mask (project+vote)    [reuse transfer + small voting pass]
  - Distill YOLO-seg on consensus               [reuse Ultralytics]
  - Leakage-free wedge nonconformity score      [NEW, small]
  - Label-free CRC calibration + UCB(delta)     [NEW, thin; MAPIE/TorchCP]

THEORY (segmentation instance ONLY)
  R_true <= R_consensus + UCB(delta). Assumptions: exchangeable object units;
  view-disjoint scoring; triangle-compatible loss (symmetric-difference, NOT IoU);
  finite-sample delta with correlated-error floor.

EXPERIMENTS (safe-accept set)
  [SPINE] label-free vs labeled-calibration risk-coverage PARITY on ScanNet/Replica GT.
  - realized risk <= alpha + UCB(delta) vs human GT; REPORT UCB width.
  - UCB(delta) width as function of n and rho; report the floor.
  - leakage sensitivity vs wedge buffer width.
  - abstention vs TRUE error in clutter.
  - public-benchmark anchor (ScanNet/Replica) for distillation quality.
  - onboard runtime/latency/memory (Jetson) vs SAM.
  - label-hours saved vs supervised parity.

KEY FIGURES
  parity curve; realized-risk-<=-alpha plot; UCB-width-vs-(n,rho) with floor;
  wedge-buffer sensitivity; SAM-parity stratified by scene difficulty.

LIMITATIONS
  consensus-bias floor under correlated SAM errors; hemisphere misses bottom geometry.

REMAINING TO-DO
  [ ] De-risk consensus->truth bound on small labeled set (GATES submission).
  [ ] Ensure reported metric = symmetric-difference end-to-end (matches theorem).
  [ ] State "matches SAM" with explicit tolerance/CI, stratified by difficulty.

PAPER P1 — SELECTIVE GRASPING: OBJECT-LEVEL CALIBRATED ABSTENTION
Venue: ICRA 2027 (~Sep 1, 2026). Score 8.0 / safe accept (unanimous).
Reuse: BITSI octree/IBR, occupancy+EDT, hemisphere->TSDF, Contact-GraspNet/GPD,
MAPIE/TorchCP. Firewall: fixed/budgeted capture, NO stopping theory; P0 = baseline.
================================================================================

ABSTRACT (claim-exact)
  Learned grasp nets output uncalibrated scores. We add a calibrated ABSTENTION
  option: an object-level conformal/RCPS certificate on OBSERVED grasp success
  gives in-distribution, finite-sample risk control on the accepted-grasp set.
  Parts are proposed by a standard medial decomposition (EDT on a fused-TSDF
  occupancy grid) and screened by a standard analytic grasp test; the contribution
  is the certificate plus an observability mask that withholds certification on
  unobserved geometry. We report held-out-object-category coverage tracking the
  nominal level, and a separate EMPIRICAL study of degradation under category
  shift. We do not claim to exceed learned nets on raw success; we report selective
  success / risk-coverage.

CONTRIBUTIONS
  1. Object-level conformal SELECTIVE grasping with in-distribution risk control.
  2. Observability-mask abstention (never certify unobserved support voxels).
  3. Grasp-specific nonconformity score with risk-coverage dominance over a
     confidence-threshold baseline.
  4. EDT medial field as a grounded part score (reusing the occupancy grid).

CLAIMS LEDGER (claim | evidence | FORBIDDEN)
  - In-distribution risk control on accepted set
      | object-level conformal on OBSERVED success; i.i.d. trials asserted;
        held-out-object coverage ~ nominal with CIs
      | FORBIDDEN: "held-out-category guarantee", per-grasp coverage
  - Category shift = STUDY, not guarantee
      | OOD degradation curve; show graceful degradation + mask catches OOD fails
      | FORBIDDEN: any "coverage"/"guarantee"/"certify" token in OOD section
  - Observability mask withholds on unobserved voxels
      | ablation: certified-on-observed vs hallucinated geometry
      | FORBIDDEN: "certifies the full object"
  - Decomposition + grasp test = standard
      | cite, mark not-novel
      | FORBIDDEN: claiming them as contributions
  - Win on selective success, not raw
      | risk-coverage AUC vs Contact-GraspNet/GPD on identical captures, with CIs
      | FORBIDDEN: "first", "beats CGN", "highest grasp success"

METHOD + CODE REUSE
  - Hemisphere -> TSDF -> occupancy -> EDT medial field r(x)   [reuse + scipy EDT]
  - BITSI decomposition as part proposer (surface-only IBR)    [reuse unchanged]
  - Analytic grasp test: friction-cone wrench-span + collision + reachability vs
    observed cloud; inflate cone by normal-estimation uncertainty   [NEW, small]
  - Observability mask                                          [NEW, small]
  - Object-level conformal selective grasping                   [NEW, thin]
  (Active recapture = engineering only; NO stopping claim.)

THEORY
  Distance-function diagram bottleneck <= Hausdorff (free). Object-level conformal
  coverage, in-distribution only. NO stopping theorem (firewall to P2).

EXPERIMENTS (safe-accept set)
  [SPINE] risk-coverage AUC vs Contact-GraspNet + GPD on IDENTICAL fused TSDF
          captures; clutter; >=200 real attempts; binomial CIs.
  [HEADLINE FIGURE] held-out-object-category empirical coverage tracking nominal
          1-alpha (with CIs and stated n).
  - OOD degradation study (graceful; observability mask catches a chunk).
  - EDT<->MAT fidelity gate (stratified by shape class) — run early.
  - registration-noise robustness of r(x).
  - observability-mask ablation.

KEY FIGURES
  held-out coverage-vs-nominal (CIs); risk-coverage AUC dominating CGN threshold;
  observability-mask abstention examples; EDT<->MAT fidelity.

LIMITATIONS
  hemisphere bottom occlusion; medial fidelity at voxel scale; in-distribution-only
  guarantee.

REMAINING TO-DO
  [ ] >=200 attempts so AUC dominance is statistically significant (CIs).
  [ ] Scrub OOD section of all coverage/guarantee/certify tokens.
  [ ] Claim risk-coverage dominance EMPIRICALLY, not as a theorem.

PAPER P2 — ACTING TO CERTIFY (THE AWARD HORSE)
Venue: RSS 2027 (~Jan/Feb 2027; draft by Dec 2026). Score 8.0 / safe accept /
FINALIST-CREDIBLE. Reuse: P1 rig/TSDF/decomposition stack, GUDHI, Open3D
free-space carving, NBV baselines. Firewall: P2 owns all active/stopping theory.
================================================================================

ABSTRACT (claim-exact)
  When has a robot seen enough? We accumulate views into a MONOTONE FREE-SPACE
  filtration whose persistence diagram provably converges: under Hausdorff-dense
  sampling, bottleneck(D_k, D_inf) -> 0, and the RETROSPECTIVE quantity
  bottleneck(D_k, D_{k-1}) gives a terminating stopping rule. This is a
  DETERMINISTIC GEOMETRIC stopping guarantee — not a probabilistic risk
  certificate. The same machinery, tracked across motion as a vineyard, discovers
  articulated parts and joint types. Stopping is near-optimal (<=eps% task-success
  loss vs exhaustive capture) using fewer views than info-gain NBV, demonstrated
  on real articulated objects.

CONTRIBUTIONS
  1. Monotone free-space filtration -> bottleneck-convergent stopping rule
     (termination + registration-robustness).
  2. Persistence vineyards for articulated-part + joint-type discovery.
  3. Near-optimal stopping vs strong NBV baselines.

CLAIMS LEDGER (claim | evidence | FORBIDDEN)
  - bottleneck(D_k, D_inf) -> 0 under stated sampling
      | convergence statement + RATE + tameness/Hausdorff assumptions
      | FORBIDDEN: vague "converges" with no target/rate
  - Retrospective stopping rule (practical criterion)
      | termination proof on monotone free-space + empirical decay curve
      | FORBIDDEN: calling it "risk control"/"distribution-free"; implying the
        retrospective increment BOUNDS residual to D_inf without increment-decay
  - Monotonicity on free-space carving
      | state assumption; report failure under depth-dropout/specular
      | FORBIDDEN: claiming surface-level monotonicity
  - Registration-robust
      | empirical robustness under injected real drift
      | FORBIDDEN: asserting it without the experiment
  - Articulation discovery
      | joint-type accuracy vs Ditto/ScrewNet
      | FORBIDDEN: "certified" articulation in the conformal/risk sense
  - "Certify" in the title
      | define explicitly as GEOMETRIC STABILIZATION, not correctness/risk

METHOD + CODE REUSE
  - Monotone free-space carving filtration from accumulated views  [reuse Open3D]
  - Bottleneck convergence stopping rule; termination; reg-robust  [NEW; GUDHI]
  - Vineyards over joint-angle/time -> part + joint type           [NEW; GUDHI]

THEORY
  Monotone filtration => nested sublevel sets => convergence; retrospective
  bottleneck(D_k, D_{k-1}) as a practical stopping criterion (justified by
  convergence + empirics, NOT a certified bound on residual error unless the
  increment-decay condition is proved). Near-optimality statement.

EXPERIMENTS (finalist set)
  [KNOCKOUT] stopping: views-to-converge and task-success-at-stop vs exhaustive,
             vs info-gain NBV (Isler), coverage, random. Report DISTRIBUTION of
             eps (view-savings & task-loss) across objects (small AND stable).
  [DEMO] repeatable, MULTI-TRIAL real articulation demo (open drawer / operate
         tool guided by temporal persistence) with success rate; joint-type
         accuracy vs Ditto/ScrewNet.
  - registration-jump robustness experiment.

KEY FIGURES
  bottleneck-decay-to-threshold; stopping vs NBV (actions saved at equal success);
  eps distribution across objects; vineyard tracking an articulating object.

LIMITATIONS
  monotonicity holds on free-space not surface; coverage-bounded scenes;
  TDA opacity for a general audience (mitigate with clear exposition).

REMAINING TO-DO
  [ ] State convergence RATE + exact Hausdorff-density condition.
  [ ] Frame stopping as practical criterion (or prove increment-decay).
  [ ] Define "Certify" = geometric stabilization in the text.
  [ ] Multi-trial demo with success rate (one-shot won't survive RSS).
