ABSTRACT
We train a fast onboard segmenter with NO human masks for training or threshold
calibration, and equip it with calibrated abstention; a SMALL human-labeled set is
used only to bound the truth-facing risk. A robot captures hemispherical RGB-D with
known poses; SAM masks fuse across views into a consensus that denoises per-view
errors; a YOLO-seg student is distilled from it. Calibration controls risk WITH
RESPECT TO the multi-view consensus under the Jaccard distance (a proper metric);
the small labeled set provides a finite-sample upper bound UCB(delta) on the
consensus-to-truth gap, yielding R_true <= R_consensus + UCB(delta). The gap delta
shrinks with view diversity DOWN TO A FLOOR set by view-correlated SAM error, which
we MEASURE. Under distribution shift we extend the transfer to
R_true^dep <= R_consensus + UCB(delta) + Gap(P_cal,P_dep), where Gap is a finite,
MEASURABLE divergence term (bounded by total variation for our metric loss), so the
truth-facing bound degrades gracefully with a measured shift budget rather than going
silent out-of-distribution; beyond an estimable budget we decline to certify. Consensus-based calibration matches annotation-based calibration on
ScanNet/Replica [MOCK: risk-cov AUC 0.86 vs 0.87, symmetric-difference loss]; the
student runs real-time onboard [MOCK: 31 FPS, Jetson Orin NX]. We do not claim to
exceed SAM; we provide a fast, abstaining student whose risk is bounded relative to
truth with minimal labels.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. INTRODUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Human masks are expensive; SAM is accurate but too slow onboard; and a robot needs
both fast masks and a reliable signal of WHEN a mask is untrustworthy. We turn a
robot's controlled multi-view capture into a calibration oracle: because camera
transforms are known, masks transferred across views must be geometrically
consistent, and their disagreement is a calibration signal requiring no human
annotation. We use this twice: (i) a multi-view CONSENSUS mask is a denoised
distillation target; (ii) cross-view disagreement, scored on view-disjoint data, is
a nonconformity score for calibrated abstention. A small human-labeled set is used
ONLY to bound the consensus-to-truth gap -- not to train and not to set the
threshold; we are explicit that the truth-facing certificate therefore needs
minimal labels.

Contributions.
 1. Multi-view geometric consensus as a calibration oracle for segmentation.
 2. A consensus-to-truth risk transfer R_true <= R_consensus + UCB(delta) under a
    triangle-compatible loss, with an explicit, MEASURED view-correlated-error floor.
 3. A leakage-free wedge nonconformity score (angularly-buffered, view-disjoint).
 4. A real-time onboard selective segmenter trained with zero human masks.
 5. A SHIFT-ROBUST extension of the transfer, R_true^dep <= R_consensus + UCB(delta)
    + Gap(P_cal,P_dep), where Gap is a finite, MEASURABLE divergence term (total-
    variation-bounded for the metric loss; equivalently importance-weight ESS),
    turning cross-distribution risk from an empirical-only study into a guarantee
    that degrades gracefully with a measured shift budget -- and that explicitly
    DECLINES to certify when the budget is unestimable (no distribution-free OOD
    coverage claim).
This paper is purely about segmentation; no cross-task claim.

consensus-truth floor, and a SHIFT-ROBUST consensus-to-truth transfer whose OOD

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. METHOD
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 Capture and consensus. K hemisphere views (known poses); SAM per view; masks
projected to a common frame and voted into consensus C(X). Consensus cancels
VIEW-DEPENDENT flicker, NOT view-correlated SAM errors (this sets the delta floor).
3.2 Distillation. A YOLO-seg student is trained on consensus masks (no human
labels). We report mask quality "within X (symmetric-difference) of SAM" with a
stated tolerance, stratified by scene difficulty -- NOT a "beats SAM" claim.
3.3 Leakage-free wedge score. For held-out view v, the nonconformity score compares
the mask in v to the consensus from an ANGULARLY-BUFFERED wedge EXCLUDING v and its
geometric neighbors; we report sensitivity to buffer width.
3.4 Calibration and loss. Using the frozen student, calibrate the abstention
threshold via conformal risk control under the JACCARD DISTANCE loss
d(A,B) = 1 - |A intersect B|/|A union B| = |A XOR B|/|A union B|. This is a PROVEN
METRIC (Jaccard/Tanimoto; the triangle inequality holds), which is what licenses
the additive transfer in Prop 1. ALL reported certified risks (Sec. 5) use this
loss; IoU and Dice are NOT metrics (triangle inequality fails) and appear only as
descriptive, non-certified asides. Object/scene-level units. The threshold controls
R_consensus; the small human set provides UCB(delta) on the consensus-truth gap.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. THEORY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Proposition 1 (consensus-to-truth transfer). With the Jaccard distance (a metric),
object-level exchangeable units, leakage-free scoring, and a frozen student,
conformal calibration gives, with high probability,
   R_true(accepted) <= R_consensus(accepted) + UCB(delta),
where the triangle inequality on the metric loss licenses the additive split and
UCB(delta) is a finite-sample upper bound on delta = E[d(C, Y)] estimated on the
human set. (The metric property is essential: IoU/Dice are NOT metrics and do NOT
satisfy the triangle inequality, so the split would be invalid for them.)
JOINT CONFIDENCE: the level-alpha CRC on R_consensus and the delta-UCB are combined
by a union bound, so the transfer holds jointly with probability
>= 1 - (delta_cal + delta_gap).

Remark (the floor). SAM errors are CORRELATED across views, so voting does not drive
delta to zero; delta decreases with view diversity only DOWN TO A FLOOR set by the
correlated-error component, which we MEASURE (Sec. 5.4). We never claim delta -> 0.

Proposition 2 (shift-robust transfer). Let calibration scenes be drawn from P_cal and
deployment scenes from P_dep. With the same metric loss (bounded in [0,1]), leakage-
free scoring, and a frozen student, weighted conformal calibration (importance weights
dP_dep/dP_cal, or the coverage-gap machinery of [Barber et al.'23]) gives, with high
probability,
   R_true^dep(accepted) <= R_consensus(accepted) + UCB(delta) + Gap(P_cal, P_dep),
where Gap is a finite, MEASURABLE inflation term. For the Jaccard distance (loss in
[0,1]) the additive gap satisfies Gap <= TV(P_dep, P_cal), a total-variation budget we
ESTIMATE (Sec. 5.7-5.8); equivalently the bound is controlled by the effective sample
size of the importance weights. When the shift is small and estimable the bound is
non-vacuous; when the shift budget is unbounded or unestimable we make NO guarantee and
report empirical degradation only (Sec. 5.7). This does NOT claim distribution-free OOD
coverage -- the guarantee is explicitly CONDITIONAL on a measured divergence, and we
report the regime where it is vacuous.

Scope. In-distribution the transfer is exact up to UCB(delta); under shift it holds up
to a MEASURED divergence budget (Prop 2), and beyond an estimable budget we report
empirical degradation only (5.7). We control risk on the accepted set; we do not
"certify the masks" in any absolute sense, and the truth-facing bound depends on the
(small) human set.

5. EXPERIMENTS  ([MOCK] numbers illustrative; all certified risks = Jaccard distance)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1 Setup. Robot hemisphere capture (no grasp campaign); SAM 2; YOLO11-seg student;
ScanNet/Replica public anchor; Jetson Orin NX for onboard timing; human-labeled
set size m=60 scenes (for UCB(delta) only).
5.2 Label-free vs labeled calibration parity (headline). Risk-coverage curves
(symmetric-difference loss): consensus-calibrated vs annotation-calibrated on
ScanNet/Replica GT. [MOCK] AUC 0.86 (consensus) vs 0.87 (labeled) -- near parity,
with CIs.
5.3 Realized-risk check. Report BOTH R_consensus and R_true vs nominal alpha and
UCB(delta), all in symmetric-difference. [MOCK] at alpha=0.10: R_consensus=0.098
(<= alpha), UCB(delta)=0.04, realized R_true=0.12 <= R_consensus+UCB = 0.138. The
bound holds because the realized gap (~0.02) is under UCB (0.04).
5.4 delta floor. UCB(delta) vs number of views and view-correlation rho; show the
floor. [MOCK] delta drops 0.10 -> 0.038 by ~8 views, then plateaus (floor ~0.034)
as rho dominates. UCB(delta) width vs human-set size m reported (power curve).
5.5 Leakage sensitivity. Realized risk vs angular buffer width; naive adjacent-view
scoring is anti-conservative [MOCK realized 0.17 at nominal 0.10]; wedge scoring
restores validity [MOCK 0.12].
5.6 Abstention vs true error in clutter; SAM-parity stratified by scene difficulty
(symmetric-difference); onboard runtime [MOCK 31 FPS @ Orin NX, latency 32 ms];
label-hours saved [MOCK ~110 h vs supervised parity].
5.7 Cross-distribution degradation + shift-budget measurement. Empirical coverage
degrades across held-out categories [MOCK] 0.83 -> 0.61; mask routes [MOCK] ~38% of
OOD failures to abstain. We ALSO estimate the shift budget feeding Prop 2's Gap term:
an estimated total variation / density-ratio between calibration and each held-out
category [MOCK: TV in 0.05-0.22], with the estimator's own uncertainty reported (the
budget must be trusted for the bound to hold).
5.8 Shift-robust bound validity (Prop 2). Realized R_true^dep vs the shift-robust
bound R_consensus + UCB(delta) + Gap across held-out categories. [MOCK] near category
(TV~0.06) at alpha=0.10: bound = 0.098 + 0.04 + 0.06 = 0.198, realized R_true^dep =
0.15 <= bound (holds, non-vacuous). Far category (TV~0.22): bound ~0.36 (holds but
loose); when TV is unestimable we DECLINE to certify and fall back to 5.7. We report
the fraction of held-out categories where the budget is estimable AND the bound holds
[MOCK: 7/9], and flag the vacuous regime explicitly.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The truth-facing bound depends on a small human-labeled set; "label-free" applies
to training and threshold calibration, not the truth bound. delta does not vanish
(correlated-error floor). Hemisphere capture misses bottom/support-occluded
geometry. In-distribution guarantee; under shift the guarantee holds only up to a
MEASURED divergence budget (Prop 2). The shift-robust bound requires an ESTIMABLE
divergence (TV / importance weights): under unbounded or unmeasurable shift we provide
no guarantee and fall back to empirical degradation (5.7), and the bound loosens as TV
grows -- becoming vacuous for far categories, which we report rather than hide. Wedge
scoring needs sufficient angular spread.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. CONCLUSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A robot's controlled multi-view capture is a calibration oracle: it distills a fast
onboard segmenter and bounds its risk relative to truth via a measured
consensus-to-truth gap -- calibrated abstention with minimal labels.
