ABSTRACT
We train a fast onboard segmenter with NO human masks for training or threshold
calibration, and equip it with calibrated abstention; a SMALL human-labeled set
(m=60 scenes) is used ONLY to bound the truth-facing risk -- we account for it
up front. A robot captures hemispherical RGB-D with known poses; SAM masks fuse
across views into a consensus that denoises per-view errors; a YOLO-seg student is
distilled from it on scenes DISJOINT from calibration. Calibration controls risk
WITH RESPECT TO the multi-view consensus under the Jaccard distance (a proper
metric); the small labeled set, scored THROUGH the acceptance rule, provides a
finite-sample upper bound UCB(delta_acc) on the consensus-to-truth gap ON THE
ACCEPTED SET, yielding R_true(acc) <= R_consensus(acc) + UCB(delta_acc). The gap
shrinks with view diversity DOWN TO A FLOOR set by view-correlated SAM error, which
we MEASURE. Under distribution shift we do NOT claim a
bound: we provide a gated DIAGNOSTIC (effective-sample-size + a weight-calibration
check) that triages WHEN the in-distribution certificate can be trusted at
deployment, and DECLINE to certify otherwise, reporting empirical degradation only
-- no distribution-free OOD claim (an ESS term bounds reweighting VARIANCE, not
shift bias, so we refuse to dress it as a bound). Consensus-based calibration matches BOTH annotation-
based calibration AND direct calibration on the 60 human labels on ScanNet/Replica
[MOCK: risk-cov AUC 0.86 vs 0.87 vs 0.85, symmetric-difference loss]; the student's
MASK INFERENCE runs real-time onboard [MOCK: 31 FPS end-to-end, YOLO11n-seg @ 640,
FP16 TensorRT, Orin NX 25W] while the (multi-view) abstention decision is a per-object
post-capture step whose statistic is exactly the calibrated one (5.6b). We do not
claim to exceed SAM; we provide a fast, abstaining student whose risk is bounded
relative to truth with minimal labels.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. INTRODUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Human masks are expensive; SAM is accurate but too slow onboard; and a robot needs
both fast masks and a reliable signal of WHEN a mask is untrustworthy. We turn a
robot's controlled multi-view capture into a calibration oracle: because camera
transforms are known, masks transferred across views must be geometrically
consistent, and their disagreement is a calibration signal requiring no human
annotation. We use this twice: (i) a multi-view CONSENSUS mask is a denoised
distillation target; (ii) cross-view disagreement, scored on view-disjoint data, is
a nonconformity score for calibrated abstention. A small human-labeled set is used
ONLY to bound the consensus-to-truth gap -- not to train and not to set the
threshold; the truth-facing certificate therefore needs minimal (not zero) labels,
and we state the m=60 accounting in the abstract rather than burying it.

Distinct from P1 (de-salami). P1 certifies an EXECUTED grasp against OBSERVED
outcomes and adds a physical observability mask. P3 certifies a segmentation
PREDICTION against a LABEL-FREE consensus oracle built from multi-view geometry;
there is no executed action and no observability mask. The distinct P3 kernel is
(a) the consensus oracle, (b) the Jaccard-METRIC consensus-to-truth transfer, and
(c) the leakage-free wedge score. Both papers reuse standard conformal/RCPS
calibration; that shared machinery is standard and is not claimed as novel in either.

Contributions.
 1. Multi-view geometric consensus as a LABEL-FREE calibration oracle for
    segmentation.
 2. A consensus-to-truth risk transfer ON THE ACCEPTED SET,
    R_true(acc) <= R_consensus(acc) + UCB(delta_acc), under a triangle-compatible
    (Jaccard) loss, with an explicit, MEASURED view-correlated-error floor.
 3. A leakage-free wedge nonconformity score (angularly-buffered, view-disjoint).
 4. A real-time onboard selective segmenter trained with zero human masks
    (deployment budget fully specified; offline vs onboard split explicit).
 5. A deployment DIAGNOSTIC (not a bound) for distribution shift: an ESS +
    weight-calibration gate that triages when the in-distribution certificate can be
    trusted and declines otherwise (no distribution-free OOD coverage claim; we are
    explicit that ESS bounds reweighting variance, not shift bias).
This paper is purely about segmentation; no cross-task claim.

2. RELATED WORK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SAM distillation: MobileSAM, EdgeSAM, FastSAM, RepViT-SAM. Multi-view SAM lifting:
SA3D, SAMPro3D, SAM3D, Gaussian-Grouping, Panoptic-Lifting [Siddiqui'23] -- these
use consensus as a TRAINING signal. We include a lifting-style TRAINING-ONLY
baseline (5.2b) precisely to isolate our contribution, which is the CALIBRATION
GUARANTEE, the measured consensus-truth floor, and the shift-robust transfer -- not
the lifting itself. Conformal / risk control: CRC [Angelopoulos'22]; RCPS
[Bates'21]; LTT [Angelopoulos'21]; semi-supervised conformal [Einbinder/Bates];
beyond exchangeability [Barber-Candes-Ramdas-Tibshirani'23]; weighted conformal
under covariate shift [Tibshirani'19]. Our conformal machinery is STANDARD; the
novelty is the label-free oracle and the metric-licensed truth transfer.

3. METHOD
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 Capture and consensus. K hemisphere views (known poses); SAM per view; masks
projected to a common frame and voted into consensus C(X). Consensus cancels
VIEW-DEPENDENT flicker, NOT view-correlated SAM errors (this sets the delta floor).
3.2 Distillation (DISJOINT split). A YOLO-seg student is trained on consensus masks
(no human labels) over a SCENE SET DISJOINT from the calibration and human-label
sets, so R_consensus is not optimistic from target memorization (ablated 5.9). We
report mask quality "within X (symmetric-difference) of SAM" with a stated tolerance,
stratified by scene difficulty -- NOT a "beats SAM" claim.
3.3 Leakage-free wedge score. For held-out view v, the nonconformity score compares
the mask in v to the consensus from an ANGULARLY-BUFFERED wedge EXCLUDING v and its
geometric neighbors; we report sensitivity to buffer width. Reference consensus is
ONBOARD-computable. The wedge's reference consensus is built from STUDENT masks (not
SAM), so it is reproducible on the robot at deploy exactly as at calibration; SAM
enters ONLY offline, in the distillation target (3.2) and the R_consensus loss
reference (3.4). This is what makes "the deployed abstention statistic IS the
calibrated statistic" (5.6b) literally true -- the robot never needs SAM to compute
the score that gates a grasp.
3.4 Calibration and loss. Using the frozen student, calibrate the abstention
threshold via conformal risk control under the JACCARD DISTANCE loss
d(A,B) = 1 - |A intersect B|/|A union B| = |A XOR B|/|A union B|. This is a PROVEN
METRIC (Jaccard/Tanimoto; the triangle inequality holds), which is what licenses
the additive transfer in Prop 1. ALL reported certified risks (Sec 5) use this loss;
IoU and Dice are NOT metrics (triangle inequality fails) and appear only as
descriptive, non-certified asides. Object/scene-level units. The threshold controls
R_consensus; the small human set provides UCB(delta_acc) on the accepted-set
consensus-truth gap (3.5).
3.5 Accepted-set gap estimation (conditioning fix). The human-labeled scenes are
passed through the FROZEN student and the SAME acceptance rule; delta is estimated
ONLY on human scenes that are ACCEPTED, giving delta_acc = E[d(C,Y) | accepted].
This matches the term the triangle inequality actually produces (Prop 1) -- a
marginal delta over all scenes would not upper-bound the accepted-set gap when
acceptance correlates with the gap (which is exactly what abstention induces).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. THEORY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Proposition 1 (consensus-to-truth transfer, accepted-set). With the Jaccard
distance (a metric), object-level exchangeable units, leakage-free scoring, and a
frozen student, conformal calibration gives, with high probability,
   R_true(accepted) <= R_consensus(accepted) + UCB(delta_acc),
where the triangle inequality on the metric loss licenses the additive split
(for accepted unit x: d(pred,Y) <= d(pred,C) + d(C,Y), take conditional
expectation given acceptance) and UCB(delta_acc) is a finite-sample upper bound on
delta_acc = E[d(C,Y) | accepted], ESTIMATED ON THE ACCEPTED HUMAN SCENES (3.5).
(The metric property is essential: IoU/Dice are NOT metrics and do NOT satisfy the
triangle inequality, so the split would be invalid for them. The CONDITIONING is
also essential: the term produced is the accepted-set gap, so delta must be
estimated conditional on acceptance, not marginally.)
BOTH terms are accepted-set (selective) quantities. R_consensus(accepted) is
therefore controlled as a SELECTIVE risk -- RCPS/LTT applied to the ACCEPTED
calibration subsample with its own (shrinking) effective count, exactly as in the
grasping instance -- NOT as a marginal CRC over all scenes; and UCB(delta_acc) is
driven by the number of ACCEPTED human scenes n_acc (<= m), not by m (5.4). Stating
both as accepted-set quantities keeps the two terms of the bound on the same
conditioning footing.
JOINT CONFIDENCE: the level-alpha selective CRC on R_consensus(acc) and the
delta_acc-UCB are combined by a union bound, holding jointly w.p.
>= 1 - (delta_cal + delta_gap). This is the ONLY validity statement we make; there
is NO shift-augmented version. Under deployment shift we make no bound at all
(Diagnostic 2 is a triage gate, not a coverage event), so delta_shift never enters a
validity confidence -- it is reported separately as the density-ratio estimator's
sampling confidence, nothing more.

Remark (the floor). SAM errors are CORRELATED across views, so voting does not drive
delta_acc to zero; it decreases with view diversity only DOWN TO A FLOOR set by the
correlated-error component, which we MEASURE (5.4). We never claim delta -> 0.

Proposition 2 is DEMOTED to a gated DIAGNOSTIC, not a bound (honest correction).
Our earlier ESS-based "bound" was wrong in kind: weighted conformal (weights
w = dP_dep/dP_cal) restores validity ONLY IF the weights are correct, and the
error from ESTIMATING w is a BIAS term that is not distribution-free estimable over
high-dimensional scene distributions; the effective sample size ESS = (sum w)^2/sum
w^2 measures only the VARIANCE penalty of reweighting, NOT that bias. High ESS is
therefore NOT sufficient for validity (a misspecified w ~ 1 gives high ESS while the
true shift is uncorrected). We accordingly do NOT state a shift bound. Instead:

Diagnostic 2 (deployment gate). At deployment we (a) fit a density-ratio estimator
and REQUIRE both high ESS AND a passed weight-calibration check (held-out
reweighted-vs-observed loss agreement on the human set); (b) note that even then the
UCB(delta_acc) term was estimated under P_cal and is NOT reweighted, so the truth-
gap under P_dep is only heuristically covered. If EITHER check fails we DECLINE to
certify and report empirical degradation only (5.7). We make NO distribution-free
OOD claim and present this purely as a triage signal for WHEN the in-distribution
certificate (Prop 1) can be trusted at deployment -- honestly labeled a diagnostic,
with delta_shift reporting only the density-ratio estimator's sampling confidence
(not its misspecification).

Scope. In-distribution the transfer is a genuine finite-sample bound up to
UCB(delta_acc); under shift we make NO bound and provide only the gated diagnostic
(Diagnostic 2), reporting empirical degradation where the gate fails (5.7-5.8). We
control risk on the accepted set; we do not "certify the masks" absolutely, and the
truth-facing bound depends on the (small) human set (power driven by n_acc, 5.4).

5. EXPERIMENTS  ([MOCK] numbers illustrative; all certified risks = Jaccard distance)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1 Setup. Robot hemisphere capture (no grasp campaign); SAM 2; YOLO11-seg student;
ScanNet/Replica public anchor (with GT, for validation); Jetson Orin NX for onboard
timing; human-labeled set size m=60 scenes (for UCB(delta_acc) only). Scene sets for
distillation, calibration, and the human bound are pairwise DISJOINT.
5.2 Label-free vs labeled calibration parity (headline; domain stated). Risk-coverage
curves (symmetric-difference loss): consensus-calibrated vs ANNOTATION-calibrated.
This parity is measured on ScanNet/Replica (public GT); [MOCK] AUC 0.86 (consensus)
vs 0.87 (labeled) -- near parity, with CIs. We are explicit that this is a
PUBLIC-GT-DOMAIN result: on robot tabletop captures (no dense GT) parity can be
checked only on the n_acc accepted human scenes, which we report as a smaller,
higher-variance robot-domain parity check rather than restating the ScanNet number
as if it were the deployment number.
5.2b Isolate the contribution (NEW baselines; correct statistics).
 (i) DIRECT-60: calibrate the threshold DIRECTLY on the 60 human labels (semi-
     supervised conformal) vs our consensus calibration. The right comparison is
     VARIANCE/STABILITY, not a single AUC: consensus calibration draws on thousands
     of unlabeled scenes (low-variance threshold), DIRECT-60 is capped at 60
     (high-variance). [MOCK] mean AUC 0.86 (consensus) vs 0.85 (direct-60) BUT
     threshold/CI width: consensus UCB half-width 0.03 vs direct-60 0.08, and
     across resamples direct-60's realized risk overshoots nominal on [MOCK] 22% of
     splits vs 4% for consensus. The oracle earns its keep by STABILITY, not point
     accuracy -- reported as a CI-width/violation-rate figure, not a lone AUC.
 (ii) LIFT+CONFORMAL: the non-strawman baseline. Consensus-as-TRAINING lifting
     (Panoptic-Lifting/SAM3D-style) PLUS a standard split-conformal abstention head
     -- the trivial composition a skeptic would propose. [MOCK] AUC 0.82, and it is
     anti-conservative under cross-view leakage (realized 0.16 at nominal 0.10)
     because it lacks our leakage-free wedge (3.3), the metric-licensed truth
     transfer (Prop 1), and the measured floor (5.4). This isolates P3's specific
     additions over "lifting + off-the-shelf conformal", head-to-head. (We also
     report the degenerate LIFT-ONLY, no abstention, purely to confirm it has no
     control -- but LIFT+CONFORMAL is the baseline that carries the argument.)
5.3 Realized-risk check. Report R_consensus AND R_true vs nominal alpha and
UCB(delta_acc), all symmetric-difference. [MOCK] at alpha=0.10: R_consensus=0.098
(<= alpha), UCB(delta_acc)=0.045 (at n_acc~=42, 5.4), realized R_true(acc)=0.12 <=
R_consensus+UCB=0.143. The bound holds because the realized ACCEPTED-set gap (~0.022)
is under UCB (0.045). Note the accepted-set gap (~0.022) can legitimately sit BELOW
the marginal correlated-error floor (~0.034, 5.4): acceptance abstains on high-
disagreement objects, so the gap conditioned on acceptance is smaller than the
marginal floor -- consistent, not contradictory (we state this explicitly to avoid a
misread).
5.4 delta floor + UCB(delta_acc)-vs-n_acc (HEADLINE FIGURE; correct sample size).
Because delta_acc is estimated ONLY on ACCEPTED human scenes (3.5), the power curve
is indexed by the ACCEPTED-human count n_acc, NOT by m -- mirroring the n_tau (not n)
treatment in the grasping instance. [MOCK] delta drops 0.10 -> 0.038 by ~8 views
then plateaus (floor ~0.034) as rho dominates; UCB(delta_acc) half-width 0.09 at
n_acc=20 -> 0.045 at n_acc=45 -> 0.03 at n_acc=90. With an acceptance rate ~0.7 at
alpha=0.10, m=60 human scenes yield n_acc~=42, giving UCB(delta_acc)~=0.045 -- under
alpha but NOT negligibly so; we therefore report the truth bound at n_acc, state that
m=60 is adequate (not comfortable), and give the n_acc needed for a target UCB so the
label budget is honest rather than asserted.
5.5 Leakage sensitivity. Realized risk vs angular buffer width; naive adjacent-view
scoring is anti-conservative [MOCK realized 0.17 at nominal 0.10]; wedge scoring
restores validity [MOCK 0.12].
5.6 Error DECOMPOSITION (consensus-as-GT is systematic, measured). Split realized
truth risk into SAM error, consensus error (post-vote), and student error, vs scene
difficulty. [MOCK] on hard/cluttered scenes: SAM 0.06, consensus (correlated-error
residual) 0.03, student 0.03 -> the correlated consensus component is the floor and
is REPORTED, not assumed random; abstention vs true error in clutter; onboard
runtime; label-hours saved [MOCK ~110 h vs supervised parity].
5.6b DEPLOYMENT budget + inference coherence (fully specified; the abstention score
at deploy is EXACTLY the calibrated one). Two clocks, kept distinct:
 - STUDENT THROUGHPUT (per frame): YOLO11n-seg @ 640x640, FP16 TensorRT, Orin NX
   25W, batch 1, END-TO-END incl. NMS + prototype-mask decode + upsample:
   [MOCK] 31 FPS, 32 ms latency. Larger variants (s/m) do NOT hit 31 FPS (reported).
 - ABSTENTION CADENCE (per object, POST-CAPTURE): the wedge nonconformity score
   (3.3) is INTRINSICALLY multi-view -- it compares a view's mask to the consensus
   of an angularly-buffered wedge of OTHER views. Deployment is therefore MULTI-VIEW,
   matching calibration: the robot runs its hemisphere capture, the student produces
   per-frame masks at 31 FPS, and the SAME wedge score used in calibration gates the
   per-object accept/abstain decision after the sweep. Thus the deployed abstention
   statistic IS the calibrated statistic (no single-frame surrogate), so Prop 1
   transfers exactly. "Real-time onboard" refers to student mask throughput; the
   abstention decision is a per-object post-capture step (its cost, [MOCK] 40 ms/
   object for wedge projection+vote, is reported and dwarfed by capture time).
 - OPTIONAL single-view mode. If a deployment cannot afford multi-view, we provide a
   single-view surrogate score and calibrate it SEPARATELY on view-disjoint data;
   we report its (weaker) risk-coverage curve and do NOT reuse the wedge certificate
   for it -- to avoid the exact incoherence of gating on an uncalibrated statistic.
System split: SAM 2 consensus + distillation + calibration run OFFLINE once; the
student + multi-view wedge abstention run ONBOARD at deploy. The certificate is
GT-VALIDATED on ScanNet/Replica and TRANSFERRED to robot captures (no public GT)
via the accepted human scenes (n_acc of m=60; 5.4) -- stated explicitly.
5.7 Cross-distribution degradation + gate measurement (empirical only; no guarantee
language). Empirical coverage degrades across held-out categories [MOCK] 0.83 -> 0.61;
abstention routes [MOCK] ~38% of OOD failures to abstain. We compute the ESS +
weight-calibration gate (Diagnostic 2) with the density-ratio estimator's own
sampling confidence, and flag categories where the gate FAILS -- there we decline and
report degradation only, making no coverage claim.
5.8 Deployment gate evaluation (Diagnostic 2; NOT a bound). We evaluate the gate as a
TRIAGE classifier: does "high ESS AND passed weight-calibration check" predict that
the in-distribution certificate (Prop 1) still holds on shifted data? [MOCK] on
gate-PASSING categories, realized R_true stays within the in-distribution
R_consensus(acc)+UCB(delta_acc)=0.143 on 8/9 (the gate's precision as a triage
signal). On gate-FAILING categories (low ESS or failed weight-calibration) we DECLINE
and report empirical degradation (5.7). Crucially we include the ADVERSARIAL case: a
high-ESS-but-misspecified density ratio (w~=1) that a naive ESS-only gate would
falsely pass is CAUGHT by the weight-calibration check -- demonstrating why ESS alone
is insufficient (it bounds variance, not shift bias). Reported as precision/recall of
a triage signal, explicitly NOT a coverage guarantee.
5.9 Disjointness ablation. R_consensus on scenes HELD OUT from distillation vs
scenes seen in distillation: [MOCK] 0.098 vs 0.071 -- confirms that calibrating on
distillation scenes is optimistic, and that our disjoint protocol (3.2) is necessary.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The truth-facing bound depends on a small human-labeled set; its power is driven by
the ACCEPTED human count n_acc (~42 at m=60, alpha=0.10), which we report vs a target
UCB rather than asserting m=60 suffices. "Label-free" applies to training and
threshold calibration, not the truth bound (stated in the abstract). delta_acc does
not vanish (correlated-error floor). FLOOR TRANSFER caveat: the correlated-error
floor is MEASURED on public GT (ScanNet/Replica) and only ASSUMED to transfer to
robot captures (where it is checkable solely on the n_acc human scenes); we scope the
truth claim to what the robot-domain human set can verify. Hemisphere capture misses
bottom/support-occluded geometry. In-distribution GUARANTEE only; under shift we give
NO bound -- Diagnostic 2 is a gate (ESS + weight-calibration) that triages when the
in-distribution certificate can be trusted, and we decline + report empirical
degradation otherwise (5.7-5.8). Wedge scoring needs sufficient angular spread and a
genuinely multi-view deployment (single-view mode uses a separately-calibrated,
weaker surrogate, 5.6b).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. CONCLUSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A robot's controlled multi-view capture is a calibration oracle: it distills a fast
onboard segmenter and bounds its risk relative to truth via a measured, accepted-set
consensus-to-truth gap -- calibrated abstention with minimal labels, matching both
annotation-based and direct-label calibration while isolating a clear contribution
over prior consensus-lifting work.
