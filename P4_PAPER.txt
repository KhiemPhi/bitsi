Part-Masked Pretraining: Geometry-Aware Self-Supervision for Point-Cloud
Transformers via BITSI Decomposition
Target: CVPR / 3DV (CV) or CoRL (robot-learning framing)
NOTE: this P4 is the LEARNED-BACKBONE paper (distinct from the earlier
"Self-Certifying Robot Perception" flagship idea, which is deferred/renamed).

ABSTRACT
Point-cloud transformers (Point-BERT, Point-MAE, PointGPT) tokenize and mask
GEOMETRY-AGNOSTICALLY: farthest-point centers + kNN balls, masked at random. We ask
whether a geometric PART PRIOR -- specifically BITSI decomposition (interior-boundary
-ratio metric + Chebyshev slice profile + octree recursion) -- yields a better
self-supervised pretext. We do NOT replace tokenization (which would inherit BITSI's
frame instability); instead we keep standard tokens and introduce PART-MASKED
autoencoding: whole BITSI parts are masked and reconstructed from the rest, plus two
geometry-grounded auxiliary objectives (slice-order prediction and IBR-profile
regression) and an octree-hierarchical masking curriculum. The result is a
manipulation-oriented backbone that improves part-centric downstream tasks and data
efficiency. On ShapeNetPart, part-masked pretraining improves fine-tuned mIoU over
random-masked Point-MAE [MOCK: +1.8 mIoU full-data, +6.3 at 5% labels], and the
backbone lifts our own downstream grasping/segmentation pipelines. We do not claim
gains on raw shape classification at scale; the benefit is part/affordance-aware
representation and label efficiency.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. INTRODUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Masked point modeling (Point-MAE) reconstructs randomly masked local patches; the
representation it learns is dominated by LOCAL surface interpolation. For downstream
tasks that are COMPOSITIONAL -- part segmentation, affordance, grasping,
articulation -- the useful structure is PARTS and their relations, which random
local masking does not target.

BITSI decomposition produces part-level, gripper-scale groupings from geometry
alone, for free (no labels). We use it as a self-supervised STRUCTURAL PRIOR. The
central design decision is what NOT to do: we do not use BITSI parts as tokens.
BITSI's parts inherit the instability of the PCA/OBB frame (a 90-degree axis flip on
near-symmetric shapes reshuffles the whole decomposition), and unstable tokens are
poison for augmentation-consistent / contrastive pretraining. Instead we keep cheap,
stable FPS+kNN tokens and use BITSI only to define the MASKING and the pretext
TARGETS -- where frame instability is tolerable (a different-but-valid part split
still yields a valid, hard reconstruction task).

Contributions.
 1. PART-MASKED autoencoding: mask whole BITSI parts, reconstruct from the rest -- a
    compositional pretext harder than random-patch masking.
 2. Two label-free geometry-grounded auxiliary objectives: slice-order prediction
    (a canonical sequence along the BITSI slicing axis) and IBR-profile regression.
 3. An octree-hierarchical masking curriculum (coarse-part -> fine-part) with
    geometrically-grounded scales.
 4. A manipulation-oriented backbone that improves part/affordance/grasp downstream
    and data efficiency, and that plugs into our grasping (P1) and segmentation (P3)
    pipelines.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. RELATED WORK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Masked/tokenized point transformers: Point-BERT (dVAE tokens over FPS+kNN),
Point-MAE (random-patch MAE), Point-M2AE (hierarchical masking), PointGPT
(autoregressive). Structure-aware tokens: Superpoint Transformer; SPT/segment-based
grouping. Structured masking (images): SemMAE, part-based / attention-guided masking.
Self-supervised geometric signals: normals/curvature prediction, contrastive
(PointContrast, CrossPoint). We contribute a PART-prior masking + geometry-grounded
pretext from BITSI, chosen to sidestep frame-instability by decoupling masking from
tokenization.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. METHOD
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 Backbone + tokens (standard). Standard point transformer; tokens = FPS centers +
kNN groups (unchanged, stable under augmentation). BITSI is applied OFFLINE and
cached per shape.
3.2 BITSI part prior (reused, standard). Per shape, run BITSI: IBR metric per slice,
Chebyshev-smoothed profile for boundaries, octree recursion into parts. Output: a
part label per point + a hierarchy (octree levels) + the 1D IBR profile + the slice
order.
3.3 PART-MASKED autoencoding. Assign each token to the BITSI part its center falls
in; MASK all tokens of a sampled subset of parts (whole-part masking, mask ratio by
part not by token); reconstruct masked parts from visible ones (Chamfer/EMD
reconstruction head as in Point-MAE). Because whole parts vanish, the model must use
inter-part structure, not local continuity.
3.4 Auxiliary objectives (label-free).
 (a) Slice-order prediction: the BITSI slicing axis induces a canonical order over
     slabs; predict the slab index of masked tokens (a geometry-grounded ordering
     signal that point transformers otherwise lack).
 (b) IBR-profile regression: regress the 1D IBR-vs-position profile (the graspability
     signature) from the visible tokens.
3.5 Octree-hierarchical curriculum. Start with coarse-part masking (shallow octree),
progress to fine-part masking (deep octree) -- multi-scale, geometrically grounded.
3.6 Frame-instability handling. Masking/targets, NOT tokens, depend on BITSI; a
frame flip yields a different-but-valid part split -> a different-but-valid pretext
instance, which is benign (even beneficial as augmentation). Tokens stay canonical.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. WHY IT HELPS / SCOPE (honest)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Helps: compositional pretext forces part-relational features; label-free geometric
targets add structure; hierarchy gives multi-scale. Expected wins: part
segmentation, affordance, grasp/articulation downstream, and DATA EFFICIENCY (fewer
labels). Scope/caveats: (i) BITSI is object-centric and gripper-scale -- gains are
expected on object-centric corpora (ShapeNet, Objaverse-LVIS), not scene pretraining
(ScanNet) without adaptation; (ii) at large scale, imposed structure need not beat
learned structure on RAW classification -- we do not claim ModelNet SOTA; (iii)
precompute cost: BITSI per shape is heavier than FPS+kNN, mitigated by offline
caching.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. EXPERIMENTS  ([MOCK] numbers illustrative)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1 Setup. Pretrain on ShapeNet (object-centric); backbone = Point-MAE transformer;
BITSI decomposition cached offline (bitsi_slicer). Compare masking strategies with
everything else fixed.
5.2 Main: part-masked vs random-masked MAE, fine-tuned on ShapeNetPart segmentation.
  Pretext                         mIoU (full) up   mIoU (5% labels) up
  From scratch                    82.1 [MOCK]      66.4 [MOCK]
  Point-MAE (random mask)         85.7 [MOCK]      74.9 [MOCK]
  Ours (part-mask)                87.5 [MOCK]      81.2 [MOCK]
  Ours (part-mask + aux + octree) 88.0 [MOCK]      82.6 [MOCK]
Largest gains in the LOW-LABEL regime (part-relational prior pays off most).
5.3 Ablations. part-mask vs random-mask (isolate the masking); +slice-order aux;
+IBR-profile aux; +octree curriculum -- each contribution's marginal mIoU.
5.4 Downstream to the program. Backbone features fed to P1 (grasping) and P3
(segmentation): [MOCK] P1 risk-coverage AUC +0.03; P3 label-free parity reached with
fewer views. Shows the pretraining is manipulation-oriented, not just a benchmark win.
5.5 Robustness / cost. Frame-flip augmentation study (part-mask is stable to it by
design); pretraining wall-clock incl. offline BITSI caching; ModelNet classification
reported as a NON-headline control [MOCK: on par with Point-MAE, no claim].

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Object-centric prior (scene pretraining needs adaptation). BITSI precompute cost
(cached). No raw-classification-at-scale claim. Gains concentrate on part-centric /
low-label downstream. Auxiliary targets (IBR, slice order) are BITSI-frame-dependent
and thus noisier than the part-mask signal.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. CONCLUSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Using BITSI as a self-supervised STRUCTURAL PRIOR -- for masking and pretext targets,
not tokenization -- yields a part-aware, manipulation-oriented point-transformer
backbone that improves compositional downstream tasks and label efficiency, and
feeds the rest of the program's certified pipelines.
